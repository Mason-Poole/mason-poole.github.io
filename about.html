About Me

Hello! My name is Mason Poole, and I've been developing software as a hobby for over ten years. A few years ago, I decided to pursue my passion for technology, and after recently earning my bachelor's degree in computer science from Southern New Hampshire University (SNHU), I'm now ready to start a long-lasting career as a web developer. I believe websites are crucial in bridging the gaps between people and information. Websites power how we develop new skills, learn new ideas, and gain a deeper understanding of the world around us, and I want to be at the forefront, shaping and innovating how society interacts with information and technology.

Throughout my programming journey, I've learned a wide array of high-demand languages, including Java, JavaScript, TypeScript, PHP, C++, C#, Python, and more. Beyond the technical components of software development, I've always had a fascination with design and the psychology behind it, striving to design programs that are more accessible, intuitive, and engaging. While my main expertise is in web and software development, I also possess strong IT skills, with certifications from Google and several years of working experience as an IT administrator at Shepherds Loft, where I was responsible for managing client emails, domains, and DNS records.

When I'm not working on a project, I enjoy spending time out in nature, mainly through camping and hiking. My love of the outdoors comes from my time in Scouts BSA, where I achieved the rank of Eagle Scout. I'm also a big music fan, constantly searching for new albums to listen to and practicing bass guitar, which I've been playing for nearly 10 years.

Reflecting on my Experience at SNHU

My time at SNHU simultaneously provided new learning opportunities and reinforced my existing knowledge of computer science, leaving me well-equipped to tackle the ever-changing landscape of web development. I expanded my understanding of data structures and algorithms by utilizing vectors, hash tables, and linked lists to develop various sorting and search algorithms, such as binary search and bubble sort. Developing a checksum verification system for an HTTPS-powered web application provided hands-on knowledge and experience with different forms of encryption, and instilled a security-first mindset into my software development process. Additionally, I've gained experience working with both SQL and NoSQL databases and integrating them into full system architectures, a key component in any full-stack web application.

The skills I've gained during my time at SNHU extend beyond the technical skills needed for software development. I've practiced writing software design documents and communicating with company stakeholders throughout several projects and during several stages of development, and have had the opportunity to work with my peers across several assignments and projects, from simulating company environments for decision making to developing a video game prototype as part of an organized team with four other students. These experiences have heightened my communication and collaboration skills across various audiences and situations, crucial skills in the fast-paced environment of software development.
